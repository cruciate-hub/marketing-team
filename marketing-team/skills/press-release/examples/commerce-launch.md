# Gold-standard example: Commerce launch (April 29, 2026)

This is the canonical reference release for social.plus. Study its tone, paragraph length, quote style, subhead phrasing, and information sequence. Match this voice and structure for product launches.

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

# social.plus Launches Commerce: Turning Community Into the New Storefront

*New capabilities bring product discovery directly into community posts and livestreams, closing the gap between high-intent conversation and action*

LONDON, April 29, 2026 — social.plus, the community infrastructure platform powering in-app social experiences for some of the world's leading brands, announced the launch of Commerce, a suite of capabilities that connects high-intent community moments to the products, services, and offerings users can actually act on, without leaving the app.

Commerce is now available across social.plus and includes three interconnected capabilities: Product Catalogue, Product Tagging in Posts, and Tag and Pin Products in Livestream.

## The gap Commerce is built to close

Community platforms generate some of the highest-intent signals in digital commerce. A member posts about a product they love. A creator holds something up on a livestream. A traveller asks where to book the hotel in the photo. These moments happen millions of times each day, yet most platforms have no infrastructure to act on them.

The result: engagement happens inside the community, but conversion happens somewhere else. The community created the intent; another platform captured the outcome.

Commerce changes this. It is the layer that connects community conversations to the products and offerings users can engage with right where the moment happened.

> "Until commerce is a core part of the community experience, justifying investment in community remains challenging. Commerce gives our customers the infrastructure to close that loop: not by bolting on a shop, but by making every high-intent moment inside the community actionable."
>
> *Amadeus Norén, VP of Marketing*

## What's new

**Product Catalogue** is the foundation of Commerce. Brands can upload products, properties, courses, or any other offerings in bulk via CSV or individually through the Console. Everything inside is instantly searchable, filterable by status, and ready to be tagged anywhere in the community the moment it is added.

**Product Tagging in Posts** allows brands and moderators to tag catalogue items directly inside any post: text, image, video, or mixed-media. Users can engage with tagged products without leaving the platform, while every view and click is captured as structured data to measure content-to-product attribution.

**Tag and Pin Products in Livestream** brings shoppable experiences to live video without disrupting the viewer. Hosts can tag up to 20 items from the catalogue and pin one as a live overlay, showing viewers the product image, title, price, and a CTA in real time. Tapping opens the product in an in-app browser while the stream continues playing. The tagged product list can be edited live and persists for replay viewers.

## Built for every industry, not just retail

While Commerce is purpose-built for retail and e-commerce brands, its architecture supports any use case where surfacing something specific within a community moment creates value. A fitness app can tag training programmes in a live workout. A travel platform can pin a property the instant it comes up in conversation. A sports operator can surface live odds during a match broadcast. A media company can tag articles and content inside editorial posts.

Commerce is designed to close the distance between intent and action, whatever form that action takes.

## Availability

social.plus Commerce is available now across the social.plus platform. Existing customers can enable Commerce through Feature Settings in the Console. For a personalized walkthrough, teams can contact the social.plus sales team at social.plus/contact/contact-sales. Full documentation is available at learn.social.plus.

## About social.plus

social.plus is the infrastructure layer for in-app community. The platform gives brands the building blocks to create owned social experiences — connection, conversation, content, and commerce — inside their own apps, with full ownership of the data, the design, and the customer relationship. Brands across fitness, travel, retail, sports, and entertainment use social.plus to make community core to how their product works. The best version of every app is Social+.

# Why this release works (notes for the skill)

**Headline.** "social.plus Launches Commerce: Turning Community Into the New Storefront"
- Colon construction: subject + outcome claim
- "Storefront" is a specific noun, not "experience" or "solution"
- 9 words, 73 characters — clean

**Subhead.** "New capabilities bring product discovery directly into community posts and livestreams, closing the gap between high-intent conversation and action"
- One sentence, 21 words
- Does the "so what" — explains what becomes possible, not what the product is

**Lede.** 56 words, one paragraph. Names what (Commerce), who (social.plus with descriptor), what it does (connects high-intent community moments to products), where (without leaving the app). Sentence 2 (in the second paragraph) lists what's included.

**Problem framing.** Three short paragraphs. Concrete vignettes ("A member posts about a product they love. A creator holds something up on a livestream."). Bridge sentence: "Commerce changes this."

**Executive quote.** 47 words, two sentences. Category claim first ("Until commerce is a core part of the community experience..."), then product positioning ("not by bolting on a shop, but by making every high-intent moment inside the community actionable"). Passes all four rules.

**Detail block.** Three components, each in its own paragraph. Each paragraph names: the component, what users can do with it, the underlying mechanism, an outcome or proof point. Structurally identical paragraphs — disciplined.

**Industry pivot.** "Built for every industry, not just retail." Four use cases in four short sentences (fitness, travel, sports, media). Closes with the reframe: "Commerce is designed to close the distance between intent and action, whatever form that action takes."

**Availability.** 4 sentences. When (now), how (Console > Feature Settings), who to talk to (sales), where to read (learn.social.plus).

**Boilerplate.** The default house boilerplate. Uses the four C's framing (connection, conversation, content, commerce) rather than a literal feature list, so it doesn't age per release. Ends with the tagline.

**Total word count.** ~580 words. Right in the product-launch sweet spot (500–800).

When in doubt about a release, ask: would this match the discipline of the Commerce release? If not, edit.
