# Release Type Playbooks

Each release type has its own lede formula, mandatory blocks, and traps. Pick the matching playbook before drafting.

---

## 1. Product launch

**When:** New product, new module, major feature suite, public availability of something previously in beta.

**Lede formula:**
> `[CITY], [Date] — social.plus, the platform powering in-app social and community experiences for leading consumer brands, announced the launch of [Product Name], [one-clause descriptor of what it does or who it serves].`

**Mandatory blocks:** All 17 from the structure template.

**Subhead patterns:**
- Problem section: "The gap [Product] is built to close"
- What's new section: "What's new" or "What's included"
- Industry section: "Built for every industry, not just [primary vertical]"

**Quote should:**
- Name the category problem
- Position the launch as closing a strategic gap (not "we built this great thing")
- Make a forward-looking claim about the category

**Traps to avoid:**
- Don't list every feature — pick 3–5 substantive ones
- Don't use "AI-powered" unless AI is the substance, not the surface
- Don't bury the availability ("available now") — it goes in Block 14, not the lede

**Example from the corpus:** social.plus Commerce launch (see `examples/commerce-launch.md`).

## 2. Funding round

**When:** Seed, Series A/B/C/D, growth round, debt facility, strategic investment.

**Lede formula:**
> `[CITY], [Date] — social.plus, the platform powering in-app social and community experiences for leading consumer brands, today announced it has raised $[Amount] in [Round Type] funding led by [Lead Investor], with participation from [Other Investors]. The investment brings total funding to $[Total] and will [primary use of funds].`

**Mandatory blocks:**
- All structure blocks PLUS:
- Investor block (after Block 10): one paragraph per major investor with a quote from the lead
- Use of funds block: 3 bullets or one paragraph naming R&D, GTM, hires

**Subhead patterns:**
- Problem section: "Why now: the community infrastructure opportunity"
- What's new: "What the funding enables"
- Industry section: "A category at the inflection point"

**Quote should:**
- CEO/founder quote talks about the category opportunity, not the investors
- Lead investor quote talks about thesis, not "excited to partner"
- Both quotes pass the lift test (see `quote-engineering.md`)

**Traps to avoid:**
- Don't disclose valuation unless explicitly cleared
- Don't claim "fastest-growing in [category]" without data
- Don't use "led by" if the lead is unclear — say "co-led by"
- Don't include investors who declined to be named

**Required disclosures (check with legal):**
- Total funding raised to date
- Board changes (new director from lead investor)

## 3. Partnership

**When:** Strategic partnership, integration partnership, GTM partnership, co-selling agreement.

**Lede formula:**
> `[CITY], [Date] — social.plus, the platform powering in-app social and community experiences for leading consumer brands, today announced a partnership with [Partner], the [partner descriptor], to [shared outcome for joint customers].`

**Mandatory blocks:**
- Skeleton 1–17 with one addition: both companies' boilerplates (Block 15 carries both, in alphabetical order)
- Joint customer benefit block (between Block 10 and 11)

**Subhead patterns:**
- Problem section: "Why customers needed this"
- What's new: "What the partnership delivers"
- Industry section: "[Combined audience] gets a unified solution"

**Quote should:**
- Co-quote: one from social.plus, one from partner
- Each quote names a specific outcome for a specific customer segment
- Quotes are NOT mirror images — each speaker says something different

**Traps to avoid:**
- Don't use "strategic" unless there's a contract structure that proves it (joint roadmap, co-marketing budget)
- Don't promise integration availability without a confirmed date
- Don't include either party's competitors in the customer-fit paragraph

## 4. Customer win

**When:** Notable customer signs, deploys, expands. Often paired with a case study.

**Lede formula:**
> `[CITY], [Date] — social.plus, the platform powering in-app social and community experiences for leading consumer brands, today announced that [Customer], the [customer descriptor], has [verbed past-tense outcome] using social.plus.`

**Mandatory blocks:**
- Skeleton 1–17 with customer as the protagonist of Blocks 7–10
- The customer quote in Block 11 carries the back half — it should be the longest quote in the release
- Customer's own boilerplate appears after social.plus boilerplate (Block 15a / 15b)

**Subhead patterns:**
- Problem section: "What [Customer] needed to build"
- What's new: "How they built it" or "The platform behind [outcome]"
- Industry section: "Why [Customer's industry] is investing in owned community"

**Quote should:**
- social.plus quote validates the customer's choice in market terms
- Customer quote tells the story of their decision

**Traps to avoid:**
- Don't name a customer without their written approval — even if the deal is signed
- Don't claim metrics ("60% engagement lift") without the customer confirming them in writing
- Don't position social.plus as the hero — the customer is the hero of their own release
- Don't include details that compromise the customer's competitive position

## 5. Executive hire

**When:** C-suite or VP-level hire, board appointment, advisory hire.

**Lede formula:**
> `[CITY], [Date] — social.plus, the platform powering in-app social and community experiences for leading consumer brands, today announced the appointment of [Name] as [Title]. [Name] joins social.plus from [previous company], where [s/he/they] [most-relevant achievement].`

**Mandatory blocks:**
- Compressed problem framing (one paragraph, not three)
- Bio block (Block 10 expanded): 60–120 words covering prior roles, key achievements, education if notable
- One quote from the new hire (forward-looking, not "honored to join")
- One quote from CEO/the manager (says why this hire, what they'll own)

**Subhead patterns:**
- Compressed structure: no problem section
- What's new: "What [Name] brings"
- Industry section: skip — go straight to availability/role-start

**Quote should:**
- New hire's quote: makes a substantive claim about the opportunity at the company, not gratitude
- CEO quote: explains the strategic rationale for the role

**Traps to avoid:**
- Don't include compensation, equity, or relocation
- Don't compare to predecessors unless intentional
- Don't include LinkedIn-style superlatives ("rockstar", "transformational leader")
- A headshot is provided separately to the newswire; the release does not reference it

## 6. Milestone / metric

**When:** Funding total, user count, customers, revenue (private companies rarely), processing volume, anniversary, geographic expansion.

**Lede formula:**
> `[CITY], [Date] — social.plus, the platform powering in-app social and community experiences for leading consumer brands, today announced [the milestone number] [unit], [time frame or comparison anchor].`

**Mandatory blocks:**
- Compressed skeleton (12–14 blocks instead of 17)
- The number IS the news — it must appear in the first 15 words of the lede
- One executive quote that says what the number means for the category

**Subhead patterns:**
- Problem section: usually skipped or compressed to one paragraph
- What's new: "What the milestone reflects" or "Inside the [number]"
- Industry section: "Where the platform goes next"

**Quote should:**
- Connect the milestone to a category claim or customer outcome
- Avoid "thanks to our customers" gratitude framing

**Traps to avoid:**
- Don't celebrate a number without a comparison ("3x year-over-year" is better than "100,000 users")
- Don't combine unrelated milestones in one release
- Don't use "industry-leading" — let the number speak

## 7. Award / recognition

**When:** Gartner placement, Forbes list, industry award, analyst report inclusion.

**Lede formula:**
> `[CITY], [Date] — social.plus, the platform powering in-app social and community experiences for leading consumer brands, today announced that it has been recognized as a [recognition name] by [recognizing body] for [criteria].`

**Mandatory blocks:**
- Compressed skeleton (10–12 blocks)
- One paragraph explaining the criteria/methodology of the recognition
- One short quote (35–50 words) from CEO/VP

**Subhead patterns:**
- Often a single subhead: "About the [recognition name]"

**Quote should:**
- Be brief
- Tie the recognition to a customer outcome or product capability — not the recognition itself

**Traps to avoid:**
- Don't reproduce the recognizing body's marketing language
- Don't claim broader market position than the recognition supports
- Be honest about the methodology — "leader" in a Magic Quadrant is different from "innovator"

## 8. Acquisition

**When:** Acquiring another company, being acquired, asset purchase.

**Lede formula:**
> `[CITY], [Date] — social.plus, the platform powering in-app social and community experiences for leading consumer brands, today announced the acquisition of [Target], the [target descriptor]. The acquisition [strategic rationale in one clause].`

**Mandatory blocks:**
- Both companies' boilerplates
- Customer transition paragraph: what happens to existing customers
- Integration/timeline paragraph: when the products combine, what changes
- Two quotes: acquirer CEO + acquired company CEO
- Financial terms disclosure block (usually "terms were not disclosed")

**Subhead patterns:**
- Problem section: "Why now"
- What's new: "What [acquired company] brings"
- Industry section: "What this means for customers"

**Quote should:**
- Acquirer quote: names the strategic gap being closed
- Acquired-company quote: makes the case for the combination from their side

**Traps to avoid:**
- Don't characterize the deal as "merger of equals" unless it legally is
- Don't speculate on layoffs/integration — say "details to be shared with affected employees"
- Don't disclose anything not in the definitive agreement
- Coordinate timing with the target company's communications team

---

## Cross-cutting reminders

For ANY release type:
- Always pick the formula that matches the news, not the one the brief leans toward emotionally
- If the brief looks like a milestone but the real news is a product capability, frame as a launch
- If the brief looks like a funding round but the real news is the use of funds (a product or expansion), the secondary news may deserve its own release a week later
- One release, one news event. If the brief contains two announcements, ask whether to split.
